# Launch Kit

## Core message

IC Copilot is not another chatbot that writes stock pitches from memory. It starts from source-linked evidence, builds an explicit causal and valuation chain, tests the counter-thesis, and can conclude that no convincing thesis exists.

## 45-second demo script

1. Open the hosted app; AAPL is already loaded.
2. Point to the IC verdict and pipeline status.
3. Open `What Changed`, then its evidence drawer.
4. Show the causal thesis graph and model-derived scenario.
5. Show Bull, Bear, and Judge.
6. Switch to BABA and mention ADR/FPI normalization.
7. End on: "Evidence before eloquence."

## Analyst launch post

> Keyword counts should not become stock recommendations. I built IC Copilot to trace an exact filing or call change through a business driver, KPI, valuation impact, counter-thesis, and monitor rule. When the chain is weak, it says no convincing thesis yet. The demos are public and require no API keys.

## Engineering launch post

> How do you use an LLM in investment research without letting it invent the thesis? IC Copilot keeps deterministic source collection, period alignment, citations, valuation, and promotion gates authoritative. LLMs may plan, triage, extract drafts, synthesize, and critique only from registered evidence IDs.

## ADR launch post

> Researching BABA is not the same as researching a domestic US issuer. The workflow needs 20-F/6-K evidence, ADS normalization, home-market peers, RMB/USD context, bilingual source preservation, and explicit gaps when point-in-time consensus is unavailable. IC Copilot now exposes that full audit trail.

## Benchmark launch post

> I published IC Copilot's research-integrity benchmark instead of asking users to trust a polished demo. It checks whether event observations retain citations and periods, map to causal drivers, include counter-theses, and produce monitorable conclusions. The benchmark is reproducible and runs without network access.

## Distribution sequence

1. Publish the release and verify the hosted demo.
2. Share the engineering post with the benchmark.
3. Share one ticker walkthrough each week for four weeks.
4. Post tailored versions to LinkedIn, X, Hacker News, relevant Reddit communities, Streamlit community channels, and finance/quant groups.
5. Ask ten analysts and AI builders for one criticism each.
6. Turn repeated feedback into labelled issues and public roadmap decisions.

Avoid buying stars, automated outreach, or cross-post spam. Optimize for qualified users who run the demo, open issues, or contribute coverage.
